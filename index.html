The AAG Digital Geographies Specialty Group (DGSG) Statement of Solidarity with the Movement for Black Lives and protestors

The board members of the Digital Geographies Specialty Group (DGSG), wish to collectively issue a statement of solidarity with the Movement for Black Lives and support anti-racist protestors, marches, and online actions. We issue this statement following the lead of, and in support of, the many AAG specialty groups also showing solidarity.

As a specialty group of the American Association of Geographers, we unequivocally support our association’s statement condemning the recent murders of George Floyd, Ahmaud Arbery, Breonna Taylor, and countless other Black Americans. We also condemn these violent, hateful, racist actions. We recognize that centuries of anti-Black policies and perversely unjust systems have embedded racism as a foundation of American society, and that many of our institutes of higher education were built on similarly racist, sexist, colonialist, homophobic, and ableist foundations. We also recognize that these issues are not contained within certain political boundaries, that racism and racist actions are present in every corner of the globe, and that the flows of these ideas and actions are accelerated through digital conduits and online communities. These structures need to change, and we commit ourselves to working together, in support of active change in our lecture halls, research teams, academic governance structures, and in our communities.

Specific actions we will take as a Specialty Group are:

-       In concert with our members and other specialty groups, organize call(s) for sessions for the 2021 AAG focused on topics of digital practices and spaces of anti-racism and racism, bias within digital space, social movements, and related topics.

-       Provide small research grants for emerging scholars working on in the area of racial justice in the context of digital geographies (see detailed call below). Those interested should submit a 1 to 2 page overview/abstract including the topic and approach by September 1st, 2020. Existing research projects are eligible. The total funds available are $1000 and may be distributed to multiple recipients.

-       Donate any remaining funds from this call for research proposals to the Black Geographies Specialty Group (BGSG) to continue its work of supporting young Black scholars and others working at the intersection of race and space.

-       Encourage DGSG members who are able to support the BGSG through direct donations and membership in that specialty group

Moving forward, we reaffirm a commitment to digital justice, that is, digital geographies that trace, envision, and enact emancipatory engagements of the digital, centering black, queer, feminist, disability, and postcolonial theories. We direct our members to read the BGSG’s Call for Transformative Racial Justice and to mobilize around its suggestions for action in their own academic communities. 

In solidarity,

The board members of the Digital Geographies Specialty Group

SMALL RESEARCH GRANT ON RACIAL JUSTICE, AAG DIGITAL GEOGRAPHIES SPECIALTY GROUP

The Digital Geographies Specialty Group is instituting a small research grant to contribute funding to current or prospective work in the area of racial justice in the context of digital geographies. The DGSG is dedicated to exploring relations between digital technologies and socio-spatial relations in their multiple manifestations. In this vein, we recognize that it is urgent to further geographical work that elucidates the linkages between digital technologies and the spatial expressions of racialized systems of domination, as well as contribute towards delineating paths for liberation, equity, and justice.

This call is a response to the worldwide wave of manifestations for racial justice, and it is also part of the DGSG’s acknowledgment of the work that we must undertake to transform our own discipline and academic institutions. For these reasons, the DGSG is committing to awarding a total of $1000 in grants to emerging geographers (graduate students, postdoctoral scholars, and non-tenured faculty) who are presently conducting or preparing to undertake work in this area. Please send proposals of 1-2 pages in length to luis.f.alvarez.leon@dartmouth.edu by September 1st, 2020. Existing research projects (with work completed during the summer of 2020) are eligible.
